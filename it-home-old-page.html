The Early Years

My first memory of ICT is the early 90s primary school computer. Personal computers where new and mysterious back then and teachers were far more at home with a blackboard than a keyboard. There was just the one machine for the entire class. We didnt have formal computer lessons, but would wait our turn to dress a teddy bear on screen.

Later on in junior school I got the chance to experiment with the roamer robot. It provideed an engaging way of exploring directions and cause and effect through ICT exploring programming, control technology and maths. You could program a roamer with the keypad on the top and then watch it roll around the corridors. You could even stick a pencil in it and it would draw where it travelled.

For Christmas in the mid 90s my parents invested in our first family personal computer the Amiga by Commodore. The operating system was AmigaDOS and the GUI was called Workbench. It came with a wide range of games and creative software for me to with. I would spend hours messing around with Deluxe Paint IV AGA (2D Image and Animation Editor) and Wordworth (Word Processor).

High School

By secondary school I was competent at loading, saving and printing my work. I would research projects using CD-ROMS like Encyclopeadia Britannica. GCSE ICT classes saw me successfully create, search, sort and merge databases.

For my final year coursework I spent a lot of my free time researching and working on my project. I designed and created a reusable secure system for managing school drama productions. It contained a data capture form, actors database, organised seating arrangements and printed tickets, flyers and posters. The project was assessed on its analysis, design, implementation, testing, evaluation and user guide. I was proud to received an overall A* grade.

At home we moved on to a desktop windows PC with dial up internet. I would play around with my own mini projects making my own comedy disks and CDs. I created excel workbooks with functions to calculate my pocket money spending.

I would make and download new objects, skins and patches for games like The Sims, effectively creating my own expansion packs. I even enjoyed poking around in the files of freeware games pasting my friends faces on to the characters, so you could play as them in the story.

I would also play around with Ring Tone Text Transfer Language (RTTTL) to transfer ringtones to my Nokia 3310. Id find the code for my favourite songs online and type it into my phone, and play around creating my own strings.

Those years also housed my first exposure to creating web pages and I thoroughly enjoyed creating my own home page using the geocities webhosting service.

University

In preparation for my undergraduate degree I purchased my first laptop. It was put to good use as I developed my IT skill set further in modules like QBIT (Quantitative Biology & Information Technology).

Throughout my assignments I regularly utilised spreadsheet, statistical analysis and presentation software and became familiar with database creation, searching and management. I was also introduced to the essentials of HTML making websites using FrontPage. I processed and analysed images and created complex a vector graphics, leading to the preparation of biological drawings.

The culmination of my developing skills where showcased in my final year project.

Specialist IT Recruitment Consultant

I became an IT Recruitment Consultant specialising in supplying development contractors to all industry sectors throughout the Midlands. I was responsible for managing the entire end to end recruitment process from requirement gathering to and closing pieces of business dealing with clients Paul Smith and Nottinghamshire Police. I successfully filled range of vacancies from Back End Database Developers to Front End Graphic Designers.

Moving Forward

As a personal interest I have taught myself search engine optimisation and have been expanding on my knowledge of HTML and CSS, making and updating websites.

In preparation for the programming aspect of my MRes I have been learning to code Perl. I have combined Perl scripts with my knowledge of HTML and CSS (using cgi) to create dynamic web pages. Using dbi I have also been able to connect my Perl scripts to databases (MySQL) by writing SQL queries into my code.

I have also been trained as a Digital Champion for Get Online Wrexham Flintshire part of an overall Digital Inclusion Strategy from the Welsh Government. The aim of the project is to address the digital divide in Wales, supporting people to break down the barriers to engage them with technology, get online and make the most of the internet. My role is to help deliver sessions, teaching basic computer skills.
